## Work Authorization And Eligibility

- The candidate is authorized to work in [country/region].
- State clearly whether the candidate needs current or future visa sponsorship.
- State clearly whether the candidate is a U.S. citizen, permanent resident, or neither if applying in the United States.
- Do not penalize postings that match the candidate's real work authorization.
- Penalize postings that require an eligibility status the candidate does not have.
- Treat generic source labels such as "H1B Sponsor Likely" or "No H1B" as metadata, not as a fit signal, unless the actual posting text confirms a restriction.
- If a role requires an existing active security clearance and the candidate does not have one, cap the score unless the posting says clearance can be obtained after hire.
- If a role requires ability to obtain a clearance, treat it as a risk unless the candidate clearly satisfies the requirement.

## Fit Priority

- First, decide whether the job is open and related to the target fields.
- Second, compare the posting against the candidate's resume, projects, skills, and realistic eligibility.
- Third, apply preferences such as role type, location, pay, and domain as score adjustments.
- Closed jobs and unrelated jobs are hard skips.

## Score Calibration

Use the full 0-100 scale:

- 0-20: closed, scam/spam, impossible eligibility mismatch, or completely unrelated.
- 21-39: very poor fit.
- 40-54: weak or mostly unrelated fit.
- 55-64: possible fit with major concerns.
- 65-74: reasonable fit worth considering.
- 75-84: strong fit worth applying to.
- 85-92: excellent fit and likely a top pick.
- 93-100: exceptional rare match.

## Strict Decision Guidance

- If the job is closed, score 0-20, priority low, recommendation skip, resume_variant none.
- If the job is unrelated to the target fields, score below 40.
- If the posting is vague or a general talent pool, cap the score unless the candidate explicitly wants that.
- Treat preferred qualifications lightly.
- Penalize required qualifications the candidate clearly does not satisfy.
- Use recommendation `apply` for strong matches, `consider` for real possible matches, and `skip` for weak or bad fits.

- Show the top jobs worth action.
- Do not include bad fits just to reach a quota.
